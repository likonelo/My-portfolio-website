About Me

From a young age, I always had a thirst for education. I was greatly influenced by my teachers and the voices of Radio Lesotho presenters. I remember how, during my childhood, my grandmother would play Radio Lesotho since morning till late at night, and that’s where my desire to learn grew stronger.

After high school, I pursued a Mass Communication course at the Institution of Extra Mural Studies (IEMS). But life had other plans, and soon after, I found myself at the National University of Lesotho campus, studying Library and Information Studies (LIS), a field I never knew existed but I now see how it’s been a path that God made possible for me and it has truly shaped my journey.

One of the most interesting parts of my LIS course has been the Web design modules. Initially, I didn’t think it was relevant to LIS, I thought “We are not here to study computer science!” But I soon realized that libraries are developing, and web design has become an essential skill in the 21st century. Thanks to these web design modules, I have learned HTML and CSS and can now create my own websites.

During my 3rd year, I had the incredible opportunity to do a 2-month attachment at Roma College of Nursing although those 2 months I spent there felt too short because I felt like I didn’t have enough time to fully apply all the skills I had learned and was eager to do more. However, this hands-on experience allowed me to put my web design skills to the test. I worked on managing new books entries into the system and creating and generating cards on the system for the book recording process.

I also completed various web design assignments that allowed me to sharpen my skills and showcase my abilities. Each project helped me grow as a web designer and I proudly submitted my work to my lecturer for evaluation.

Professional Projects

Roma College of Nursing | 2024

In 2024, I was attached at Roma College of Nursing Library. During that two-month attachment, I applied practical web and library skills to improve efficiency and user experience. My responsibilities included the following tasks:

  • Recording books into the library system
  • Cataloguing books for easy retrieval
  • Creating detailed book cards for each entry
  • Assisting in the management of library resources
  • Implementing best practices for library organization

High School Library Support | 2023

As a member of the Lesotho Library Association, I joined others in visiting St Agnes, Sechaba and Kolonyama high school libraries and I assisted with the following activities:

  • Organized and classified library collections
  • Delivered practical training on cataloguing methods

Case Study: State Library of Lesotho | 2025

In order to examine whether the State Library of Lesotho meets the needs of people with disabilities, I conducted a case study focusing on three special groups:

  • the visually impaired
  • individuals with mobility impairments
  • those with hearing impairments

During my study, I discovered that the State Library, with support from Vodacom Lesotho, had made significant improvements in the section serving visually impaired users.

Vodacom provided computers and other assistive equipment to enhance accessibility.
